Understanding Watch Movement Types

There are three main types of watch movements: quartz, mechanical, and automatic. Each has its own charm and purpose. Quartz is battery-powered and highly accurate, mechanical requires manual winding, and automatic winds itself through wrist movement. Knowing the difference helps you choose the right watch for your lifestyle.

How to Choose the Right watch size?

Watch size is measured by the diameter of the case in millimeters. Smaller wrists usually look best with 38 - 40mm cases, while larger wrists can carry 42-46mm comfortably. A watch that fits well feels balanced and does not slide around or feel too tight on your wrist.

Water Resistance Ratings Explained

Water resistance is shown in meters or ATM on the watch dial or case back. A watch rated 30m is splash-proof only. 100m is suitable for swimming, and 200m or more is designed for diving. Always check the rating before wearing your watch near water to avoid accidental damage.

Watch Strap Materials and Their Uses

Watch straps come in leather, rubber, metal, and fabric. Leather straps look elegant and suit formal occasions, but should not get wet. Rubber straps are durable and great for sports. Metal bracelets are sturdy and work well for both casual and formal wear. Choose a strap that matches your daily activities.

When to Service Your Watch

Most watches should be serviced every 3 to 5 years to keep them running accurately. Signs that your watch needs servicing include losing or gaining time, a foggy crystal, or difficulty winding. Regular servicing by a professional can prevent bigger problems and keep your watch in great condition for years to come.
